How to Calculate Freelance Hourly Rate

Calculating your freelance hourly rate starts with understanding how much money you want to earn and how many hours you can realistically work. Unlike salaried jobs, freelancers must account for unpaid time, platform fees, and irregular workloads.

First, set a clear monthly income goal based on your living expenses, savings, and financial priorities. Next, estimate how many hours per week you can spend on paid client work, not total working hours.

Then, decide how many weeks you work per month. Most freelancers use four weeks, but this may vary depending on time off and workload. Finally, include platform fees so your rate reflects your actual take-home pay.

Common Mistakes Freelancers Make When Pricing Hourly

Many freelancers struggle with pricing, not because they lack skills, but because they make a few common mistakes when setting their hourly rate.

Why Do Freelancers Often Undercharge Their Hourly Rate?

One of the most common mistakes freelancers make is undercharging their hourly rate. This often happens because they compare themselves to salaried employees or other freelancers without understanding the full cost of freelancing.

Freelancers usually have unpaid time for client communication, marketing, revisions, and administration. When these hours are ignored, the hourly rate may look acceptable on paper but result in much lower real income.

How Many Billable Hours Should Freelancers Actually Count?

Another common mistake is overestimating the number of billable hours per week. Many freelancers assume they can bill 40 hours per week, similar to a full-time job, but this is rarely realistic.

Time spent on emails, meetings, finding new clients, and managing projects is usually unpaid. Failing to account for this leads to an hourly rate that does not support long-term income goals.

Forgetting Platform Fees and Unpaid Time

Many freelancers forget to include platform fees and unpaid work time when pricing their services. Platforms often take a percentage of each payment, which directly reduces take-home pay.

Ignoring these factors often results in freelancers working more hours while earning less than expected.

Frequently Asked Questions About Freelance Hourly Rates

How much should I charge as a freelance hourly rate?

There is no single correct freelance hourly rate. The right rate depends on your income goals, available working hours, experience level, and expenses. A structured calculation based on your monthly target income is more reliable than copying average rates.

Is $25 per hour a good freelance rate?

A $25 hourly rate may be reasonable for some freelancers, but it depends on workload, location, and platform fees. For many freelancers, this rate can become unsustainable if unpaid time and commissions are not included.

How many hours should freelancers bill per week?

Most freelancers cannot bill 40 hours per week consistently. Billable hours are usually lower due to client communication, marketing, and administrative work. Estimating realistic billable hours helps prevent underpricing.

Should freelancers charge hourly or per project?

Both pricing models can work. Hourly pricing offers transparency, while project-based pricing provides clearer expectations. Many freelancers use hourly calculations to set a minimum rate, even when pricing projects.

What is the average freelance hourly rate?

Average freelance hourly rates vary widely by skill, experience, and market. Relying on averages alone can be misleading. Calculating a personalized rate based on your own goals and constraints is usually more effective.
